From Ashes to Color

The Mythos

01

The Tyrant King

Before the colors, there was a dark reign. Hiranyakashipu, a demon king, conquered earth and commanded everyone to worship him as a God. His power was absolute, yet his ego was fragile.

02

The Devout Son

The king's own son, Prahlad, refused. He remained a steadfast devotee of Lord Vishnu. This defiance enraged the King, who saw his son’s devotion not as piety, but as treason punishable by death.

03

The Fire Plot

After many failed attempts to kill Prahlad, the king turned to his sister, Holika. She possessed a magical shawl that made her immune to fire. She tricked Prahlad into sitting on her lap within a roaring pyre.

04

Triumph of Good

As fire consumed them, Prahlad prayed. A divine wind transferred the shawl from Holika to the boy. Holika perished in the flames she thought she controlled. This victory of devotion over deceit is celebrated as the pyre of Holi Eve.
